Public transport for New Year's Eve 2022

Public transport is free from 6pm and running all night on New Year's Eve, and is still the best way to get around Melbourne welcome in the new year!

Before you travel

Plan your journey using Journey Planner and check Disruptions for service changes that may affect your journey.

Free travel  

Metropolitan trains, trams, buses and regional town buses will be free from 6pm on New Year's Eve until 6am on New Year’s Day.

V/Line travel will be free on services where touch on occurs after 6pm on New Year’s Eve and 6am on New Year's Day. Travel is also free on the first timetabled V/Line service departing Melbourne on New Year's Day for regional Victoria.

Passengers do not need to travel with a valid myki during periods of free travel. Metropolitan ticket barriers will be open at all stations. Passengers who touch on their myki after 6pm will not be charged. If passengers touch on before 6pm, they should make sure to touch off or they may be charged a default fare.

Passengers leaving Melbourne on the first timetabled V/Line services on New Year's Day should avoid touching on, otherwise a myki money fare will apply. In the event that passengers are charged to travel on the first timetabled V/Line service leaving Melbourne, passengers are advised to call our call centre.

Services running all night

Metropolitan trains and trams (except Routes 78 and 82) will run all night to help you travel to your New Year's Eve event and back again.

New Year's Day

Most services will be running to a Sunday timetable on New Year's Day.

 Metropolitan bus

There'll be no additional services to bus routes in the city on New Year's Eve.

Route 605 buses will be diverted due to the closure of Birdwood Avenue and Linlithgow Avenue. These services will be diverted from first service and will travel along Alexandra Avenue, City Road, resuming normal service from Power Street missing Anderson Street, Domain Road, Birdwood Avenue, Linlithgow Avenue, and Southbank Bouveland until last service. 

Route 234 (Garden City to City – Queen Victoria Market) and 236 (Garden City to City – Queen Victoria Market via City) buses will be diverted due to the closure of Flinders Street from 5pm. These services will be diverted from 5pm and will travel along City Road, Clarendon Street, Collins Street into Queen Street resuming to normal service, missing Flinders Street (William Street to Queen Street) and Queen Street (Flinders Street to Collins Street) until last service. 

Buses will run to the normal Saturday timetable on Saturday 31 December. Night Network buses will run.
