Public transport for Port Fairy Folk Festival: Friday 7 March to Monday 10 March 2025

Events > Culture

Get ready for the Port Fairy Folk Festival returning to Southcombe Park in Port Fairy in 2025! There will be additional services on Monday to get you home from the event.

Southcombe Park and Port Fairy

  • Event starts at 4pm Friday 7 March

  • Event ends at 2pm Monday 10 March

Before you travel

There will be changes to road conditions for the festival and this will impact on bus services around Port Fairy. Please see below for further information on service changes.

Bookings are essential for V/Line trains to Warrnambool to connect with services to and from Port Fairy and attendees planning to catch a train are advised to plan ahead.

Passengers must reserve a seat in advance to travel on V/Line services to Warrnambool. Reservations can be made online on the V/Line website and mobile app, by calling 1800 800 077 or in person at a V/Line Staffed Stations, V/Line ticket agent or a Metro premium station. 

A V/Line paper ticket is required to travel to and from Warrnambool, for further information on regional fares, visit the V/Line website here

To reserve your seat onboard V/Line train services, visit the V/Line website here

Plan your journey using Journey Planner and check Disruptions for service changes that may affect your journey.

For further event information, please visit the Port Fairy Folk Festival website here.

Regional train

Getting to Port Fairy

Passengers can catch regular services to Warrnambool Railway Station and then connect with bus and coach services to Port Fairy.

Leaving Port Fairy

Passengers can catch regular bus and additional coach services from Port Fairy and then connect to regular trains departing Warrnambool Railway Station.

To assist patrons leaving the event, VLine will run additional coach services between Port Fairy and Warrnambool on Monday 10 March 2025, departing:

  • Port Fairy to Warrnambool departing 10.20am
  • Port Fairy to Warrnambool departing 4.32pm

Regional bus

Getting to Port Fairy Folk Festival

Passengers can catch a Festival Bus Shuttle running between Warrnambool and the Festival, and Gum Tree Caravan Park and the Festival.

There is also a Festival Shuttle Bus that does a circuit of the town every 30 minutes, finishing at the Festival Arena. This bus operates regularly throughout the Festival on a gold coin donation. The Shuttle Bus is a low floor bus with pram and wheel chair access.

For further information regarding free shuttle bus dedicated bus stops, please see the event website.

Regular Bus Service Changes

  • Route 08 Warrnambool – Port Fairy

  • VLine Mt Gambier to Melbourne via Warrnambool and Geelong coaches.
