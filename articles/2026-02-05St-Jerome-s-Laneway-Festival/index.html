St Jerome’s Laneway Festival

Public transport for the St Jerome’s Laneway Festival at the Flemington Racecourse on Friday 13 February.

The event will run from 12pm to 11pm.
 

Before you travel

If you use Journey Planner, you'll see all the extra services we've added to help you get there.

Metropolitan train

Getting to and from Flemington Racecourse

Trains will operate from Flinders Street (Platforms 10) in both directions, stopping at Southern Cross (Platforms 13/14) and North Melbourne (Platforms 5/6), then express to Flemington Racecourse (Platform 1).

The last train to depart Flinders Street will be at 3:30pm. 
Please note there will a break in service between 3:35pm and 7:15pm. 
Trains will recommence from Flemington at 7:20pm.


Trains will operate from Flemington Racecourse (Platform 1) in both directions, running express to North Melbourne (Platform 5/6), Southern Cross (Platform 13) and Flinders Street (Platform 10)).

The final train departing Flemington Racecourse towards Flinders Street will be at 12:25am on Saturday.

Metropolitan tram

Regular Trams will operate via Route 57 between Stop 01 Flinders Street / Elizabeth Street and Stop 30: Flemington Drive/ Epsom Road, for patrons wishing to make their way to and from the event. 

Metropolitan bus

Getting to and from Flemington Racecourse

The following bus route travels close to Flemington Racecourse: 

  • Route 472 (Williamstown to Moonee Ponds via Footscray) (please note service may finish prior to the end of the music festival)

Passengers can catch buses from Footscray Station and alight at Langs Road/Showgrounds. 

Regional train

Getting to and from Flemington Racecourse

Passengers arriving at Southern Cross station are advised to change trains and catch a Flemington Racecourse service. Services will return then via Southern Cross Station. 

Refer to the Metropolitan trains section above for service times and platform information.
