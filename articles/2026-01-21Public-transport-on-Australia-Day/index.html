Public transport on Australia Day

On public holidays, daily myki fare caps apply ($8 for full fare and $4 for concessions and seniors), giving you unlimited travel for the day as usual.

For more ticketing information, including myki, regional travel and fare caps, visit Tickets and myki – Transport Victoria.

Metropolitan train

  • Metro trains will run to a Saturday timetable.
  • Night Network services will not run.
  • The Metro Tunnel is now open – see the Metro Tunnel service information page for details.

Summer Start weekend timetable (30 November 2025 to 1 February 2026)

  • Metro Tunnel services run every 20 minutes between Westall and West Footscray from 10:00am to 7:00pm.
  • Some services extend to:
    • East Pakenham every 40 minutes, and
    • Sunbury every 60 minutes.
  • All other trains on the Cranbourne, Pakenham, and Sunbury lines continue to run through the City Loop on their regular timetable.

Metropolitan tram

  • Trams run to a Saturday timetable.
  • Night network services will not run.

Australian Open event tram services

  • Australian Open shuttle trams run as Route 70a between Stop D6 Flinders Street West and Stop 7C MCG Gates 4-7 / John Cain Arena, until one hour after the end of play each night.
  • Event trams also run as Route 70a after the end of play each night between Flinders Street Station and Stop 39 Camberwell Tram Depot
  • Australian Open shuttles are free for event ticket holders and accredited staff.
  • Find out more on the Australian Open 2026 page.

Regional train and coach

V/Line services will run to a Saturday timetable for the Australia Day public holiday on Monday 26 January, except:

  • Swan Hill Line trains will run to a Sunday timetable.

Bendigo Line

  • The 3:47pm Eaglehawk to Southern Cross train will depart from Eaglehawk later at 6.48pm. This is due to the service originating at Swan Hill and running to a Sunday timetable.

Geelong – Apollo Bay

  • Apollo Bay Summer timetable will run.

Night Coaches

  • All night coaches will not run.

Metropolitan and regional bus

  • Metropolitan buses will mostly run to a Saturday timetable
  • Regional bus timetables will vary by operator.
  • Night network services will not run.
