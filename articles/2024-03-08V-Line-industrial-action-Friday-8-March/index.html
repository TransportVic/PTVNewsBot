V/Line industrial action Friday 8 March

News > Disruption

Rail Tram and Bus Union (RTBU) members employed by V/Line will take protected industrial action in the form of a work stoppage from 3am to 4pm on Friday 8 March 2024.

No V/Line trains will run during the stoppage. Significant delays are expected for the entire day.

Metro trains, trams and buses will run as normal. Roads are expected to be very busy.

V/Line trains will progressively return from 5pm but delays and cancellations will continue across the evening.

We encourage passengers to avoid travel or consider other transport where possible.

Schools will be notified directly with information about service changes and travel advice for students.

Additional services for special events are scheduled to run as planned but passengers are encouraged to check the V/Line or PTV websites before travelling.

Passengers who need to travel should expect significant delays and plan their day accordingly.

Freight trains will continue to operate during the planned industrial action so always obey warning signs at level crossings.

Essential travel from first service to 6pm

An extremely limited coach replacement service will run for essential journeys from first service to 6pm.

Coaches will not stop at any Metropolitan stations.

Delays of up to 2 hours or more are expected before 6pm.

Geelong and Warrnambool lines

An extremely limited number of coaches will run between Southern Cross, Geelong and Warrnambool. Coaches will not stop at Footscray and Sunshine.

Wyndham Vale Station

A limited number of buses will run between Wyndham Vale and Werribee to connect passengers with Metro services on the Werribee Line.

Tarneit Station

A limited number of buses will run between Tarneit and Laverton to connect passengers with Metro services on the Werribee Line.

Ballarat and Ararat lines

An extremely limited number of coaches will run between Southern Cross, Ballarat, Ararat and Maryborough. Coaches will not stop at Footscray and Sunshine.

Bendigo Line

An extremely limited number of coaches will run between Bendigo, Echuca and Swan Hill and Watergardens Station on the Sunbury Line. Passengers will need to transfer to or from a Metro Trains service to continue their journey to and from Southern Cross. Coaches will not stop at Sunbury Station.

Traralgon and Bairnsdale lines

An extremely limited number of coaches will run between Traralgon, Bairnsdale and Pakenham. Passengers will need to transfer to or from a Metro Trains service to complete their journey to and from Southern Cross.

Other important information

Passengers with accessibility needs are encouraged to call 1800 800 007 before you travel so we can provide the best possible service for your journey.

Metropolitan, freight and the Sydney-Melbourne XPT train services will still operate during planned industrial action. Following the action, there will be V/Line train movements from 5pm, so please always obey warning signs at level crossings.
