Public transport for Labour Day on Monday 8 March 2021

Events > Public holiday

The Labour Day public holiday falls on Monday 8 March.

Public transport services will run to a public holiday timetable, but some services may be altered or will not run.

Plan your journey using Journey Planner or view timetables or your specific line or route before you travel.

Before you travel

We strongly encourage you to register your myki so that we can contact you if you have been exposed to COVID-19 on a public transport service. Register your myki now - itâ€™s quick and easy.

When travelling on public transport you must wear a fitted face mask covering your nose and mouth, unless you have a lawful reason for not doing so, and ensure you wear it for your entire journey.

Metropolitan train

Trains will run to a Saturday timetable, with no Night Network services.

Please note that there may be planned works that could affect your travel. Visit our Disruptions page for more information.

Plan your journey using Journey Planner or view timetables.

Metropolitan tram

Trams will operate to a Saturday frequency with no Night Network services.

This means that if your tram normally runs every seven minutes, it will continue to do so. However, times may differ slightly, with last tram approximately one hour earlier than the normal Saturday timetable.

City Circle trams run to a normal Monday timetable.

Plan your journey using Journey Planner or view timetables.

Metropolitan bus

Most services will run to a Saturday timetable, but some services may be altered or will not run. Please plan your journey using Journey Planner or view timetables for your specific route.

Regional train and coach

V/Line rail and coach services will run to an altered Saturday timetable.

Track works on the V/Line network over the long weekend will mean that some train services will be replaced by coach for all or part of the journey. Visit our Disruptions page for more details.

Plan your journey using Journey Planner or view timetables.
