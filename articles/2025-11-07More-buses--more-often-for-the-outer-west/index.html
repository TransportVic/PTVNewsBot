More buses, more often for the outer west

On 7 December 2025, we’re introducing new bus routes 154 and 194, and upgrading Route 153, delivering more than 1,500 new weekly trips across the three routes.

It’ll be easier to get to trains, other bus routes, schools, jobs and shopping across Werribee, Tarneit, Truganina, Laverton, Harpley, Mambourin, Cornerstone Estate, Hoppers Crossing, Williams Landing and Wyndham Vale.

There will also be some bus stop changes in Tarneit and at Tarneit Station.

These improvements build on changes made in the outer west in July, when we extended operating hours and added 470 extra weekly trips across routes 170, 180, 190 and 192.

Route 153 Williams Landing Station – Werribee Station via Princes Highway

  • Route 153 is getting 394 extra weekly trips and longer operating hours, seven days a week.
  • You will have better access to the new Wyndham Law Courts, Werribee Hospital and the University of Melbourne and Victoria University Werribee campuses.
  • You’ll also have better connections to buses and trains at Werribee, Hoppers Crossing and Williams Landing stations.
  • A new eastbound stop will open on Princes Highway, which is a short walk from the new law courts.
  • The improved route will make it easier to get to jobs and shopping at Hoppers Crossing Shopping Centre, Hoppers Crossing Homemakers Centre and Williams Landing Shopping Centre.
  • Buses will run every 20 minutes for most of the day, seven days a week.
  • On weekdays and Saturdays, buses will run later until midnight.
  • On Sundays, buses will run later until just after 11 pm.

Route 154 Tarneit Station – Laverton Station via Truganina

  • We’re introducing a new bus service between Tarneit, Truganina and Laverton.
  • Route 154 will connect you to metro trains at Laverton Station and V/Line trains at Tarneit Station.
  • Route 154 will give you better access to jobs, schools and shopping at Truganina Central Shopping Centre.
  • Route 154 will travel to new parts of the Truganina and Laverton North industrial areas, making it easier for you to get to work on public transport.
  • Route 154 will run 728 trips a week.
  • Route 154 will run every 20 minutes for most of the day, seven days a week.
  • On weekdays, Route 154 will run from 5 am to 12 am.
  • On Saturdays, Route 154 will run from 6 am to 12 am.
  • On Sundays, Route 154 will run from 6 am to 11 pm.

Route 194 Werribee - Wyndham Vale Station via Harpley and Mambourin

  • We’re introducing a new bus service between Wyndham Vale, Mambourin and Harpley Estate.
  • Route 194 will give Harpley, Mambourin and Cornerstone Estate bus services for the first time.
  • Route 194 will connect locals in Harpley, Mambourin and Cornerstone Estate to V/Line trains at Wyndham Vale Station for the first time.
  • Route 194 will make it easier for you to get to jobs, schools and shopping at Mambourin Marketplace and Harpley Town Centre.
  • Route 194 will run 463 trips a week.
  • On weekdays, Route 194 will run every 20 minutes from 6 am to 9 am and 3 pm to 9 pm. Buses will run every 40 minutes at other times.
  • On weekends, Route 194 will run every 40 minutes.
  • On weekdays, Route 194 will run from 5:35 am to 10:30 pm.
  • On Saturdays, Route 194 will run from 7:20 am to 10:20 pm.
  • On Sundays, Route 194 will run from 7:20 am to 10:20 pm.

Bus stop changes in Tarneit

  • The bus stop on Derrimut Road, next to Tarneit Central Shopping Centre, will reopen and be used by routes 150, 151, 167 and 170. This makes it easier for you to get to Tarneit Central Shopping Centre.
  • Routes 150, 151, 167 and 170 will use the northern bus interchange at Tarneit Station, instead of the southern bus interchange.
    o    Route 150 will use Bay I
    o    Route 151 will use Bay J
    o    Route 167 will use Bay K
    o    Route 170 will use Bay L.
  • Routes 152, 160 and 182 will change bus bays at the southern bus interchange at Tarneit Station.
    o    Route 152 will move to Bay B
    o    Route 160 will move to Bay H
    o    Route 182 will move to Bay C.

Bus stop closures 

Due to ongoing road and construction work, some bus stops on Route 154 won’t be ready when the route starts on 7 December 2025. 

Stops closed on Route 154

  • Two stops on Dohertys Road, near Forsyth Road (stop ID’s 29099 and 29100) will be closed until late 2026.
    • Passengers can use alternative stops on Dohertys Road, near Niton Drive (stop ID’s 29101 and 29102).
  • Two stops on Leakes Road (stop ID’s 29115 and 29117) will be closed until mid-December 2025.
    • Passengers can use alternative stops on Leakes Road and Foundation Drive (Stop ID 29113) and Old Geelong Road and Tarran Street (stop ID 11541).

Passengers should plan ahead before traveling.
