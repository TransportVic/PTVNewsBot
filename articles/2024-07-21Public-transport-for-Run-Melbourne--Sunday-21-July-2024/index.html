Public transport for Run Melbourne: Sunday 21 July 2024

Events > Culture

Run Melbourne will return to the Grand Slam Oval, Melbourne Olympic Park on Sunday 21 July, commencing at Batman Avenue.  

Participants can take part in the following running events:

  • The Half Marathon Run at 6:25am
  • The 10 km Run at 8:55am
  • The 5 km Run at 10:05am

Public transport is the best way to get to the event as parking will be restricted and road closures for the marathon will be in force from 3am until 1:30pm Sunday 21 July.

Please visit the Run Melbourne website for further event and road closure information.

There are also road closures on La Trobe Street between Harbour Esplanade and Queen Street due to tram stop upgrade works. Learn more about La Trobe Street tram stop upgrades.

Before you travel

Passengers, please note there will be disruptions on the MTM network for the Frankston and Mernda lines due to scheduled maintenance works.

  • Bus replacement services will operate Cheltenham to Frankston / Stony Point on the Frankston Line
  • Bus replacement services will operate Parliament to Reservoir and Macleod on the Mernda Line

There will be disruptions on the Yarra Trams network due to the Run Melbourne road closures for routes 11, 12, 48, 109, 70, 75, 30, 35, 86, 96, 58, 1, 3/3a, 5, 6, 16, 64, 67 and 72, with diversions and stop closures in place. Bus replacements will run for passengers on nominated tram routes.          

Due to the La Trobe Street tram upgrade works, there will be no trams running between:

  • Central Pier / Harbour Esplanade (Stop D2) and Melbourne Central Station / Elizabeth St (Stop 5) on Routes 30 and 35 (City Circle).
  • Central Pier/Harbour Esplanade (Stop D2) and Stop 119 on Route 86.

There will also be disruptions on the metropolitan bus network for routes 234 and 235 diverting from first service to 9:30am (both directions) and route 605 diverting from 8am to 1pm.

Plan your journey using Journey planner and check Disruptions for service changes that may affect your journey.

 Metropolitan train

Regular train services will operate to Flinders Street Station. Passengers are advised to disembark at Flinders Street Station or Richmond Station and make their way on foot towards the start line on Batman Avenue.
