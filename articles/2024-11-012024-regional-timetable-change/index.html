2024 regional timetable change

From 1 December 2024, a new regional train timetable will begin on the Geelong, Warrnambool and Ballarat lines. The new timetable delivers more services and reliable trains across Victoria. There will be changes to bus and coach timetables to match the train timetables.

Passengers should check their timetable and plan ahead.

Geelong Line changes

  • Four additional weekday services will be added at Waurn Ponds Station.
  • 15 additional weekday services will be added at Marshall Station.
  • 68 new weekend services will be added to the Geelong Line between Melbourne and Waurn Ponds, with trains running every 20 minutes between 8 am and 9 pm. Also, 20 Wyndham Vale services will be extended to Waurn Ponds on weekends.
  • The departure and arrival times for some Wyndham Vale services will change and larger nine-car VLocity trains will run on select peak services to provide more seats.
  • There will be changes to when trains stop at Corio, North Shore, Marshall, South Geelong and Waurn Ponds stations. This means trains will stop at different stations at different times.
  • There will be journey time changes for some services.

Warrnambool Line changes

  • A new weekend Warrnambool service will be added, providing four return services a day on weekends.
  • VLocity trains will replace most older trains on the Warrnambool Line, offering more reliable journeys on modern fleet.

Ballarat Line changes

  • Five morning and five afternoon peak Bacchus Marsh services will have journey time savings of three to five minutes.
  • Three peak weekday services that end at Melton will be extended to Bacchus Marsh, providing additional direct services to Bacchus Marsh from the city.
  • There will be journey time changes for some Ballarat services.

Albury Line changes

  • Two weekend services will be permanently added to the timetable. These services have been running as specials following the introduction of the regional fare cap.

Bendigo Line changes

  • Two weekend services will be permanently added to the timetable. These services have been running as specials following the introduction of the regional fare cap.

Geelong coach changes

  • The 7.56 am coach between Geelong and Marshall and the 6.08 pm coach between Marshall and Geelong will be removed due to duplication with train services. Passengers can now catch the train between Geelong and Marshall at these times instead of the coach.

Ballarat coach changes

  • The 5.19 pm and 7.04 pm coach services between Ararat and Ballarat will be removed due to duplication with train services.
  • On weekends, the Ararat to Ballarat service will be extended to start in Horsham, becoming the Horsham to Ballarat coach. This service departs at 7.34 am on Saturdays and 8.34 am on Sundays.
  • On Saturdays, the 11.56 am Ballarat to Ararat service will also be extended to start in Horsham, becoming the 11.53 am Ballarat to Horsham coach.
  • The Saturday Ararat to Ballarat service will be extended to start in Horsham, becoming the 12.28 pm Horsham to Ballarat coach.

Albury coach changes

  • The weekday 6.23 am Bright to Wangaratta coach will depart from Bright, Porepunkah, Myrtleford and Wangaratta City 5 minutes earlier than the current timetable. The service will arrive at Wangaratta 5 minutes earlier at 7.23am.
  • The weekday 6.33 am Corowa to Wangaratta coach will depart from Corowa, Wahgunyah, Rutherglen, Springhurst and Wangaratta City 5 minutes earlier than the current timetable. The service will arrive at Wangaratta 5 minutes earlier at 7.23 am.
