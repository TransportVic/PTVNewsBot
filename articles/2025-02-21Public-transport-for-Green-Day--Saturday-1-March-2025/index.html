Public transport for Green Day: Saturday 1 March 2025

There will be disruptions across the public transport and road network.  Check for public transport and road disruptions before you go!  Please allow extra time travelling to and from Marvel Stadium. 

Before you travel

  • Plan your journey using Journey planner. Check Disruptions for service changes that may affect your journey.
  • Check for road closures around Marvel Stadium.

Getting to the concert by train 

With gates opening at 5pm, please note there will be replacement buses to and from the event on Saturday 1 March between: 

  • Heidelberg to Eltham  
  • Parliament to Caulfield / Sandringham 
  • Burnley to Caulfield 

Getting home from the concert by train 

  • Hurstbridge passengers are advised to board a Heidelberg train at Southern Cross (Platform 9) and change at Heidelberg for a bus. 
  • Alamein passengers are advised to board a Ringwood train at Southern Cross (Platform 10) and change at Camberwell for a bus.  
  • Werribee passengers are advised to board a Newport train at Southern Cross (Platform 14) and change at Newport for a bus. 

Further details below on the extra services and other changes for this weekend. 

 Metropolitan train

Bus replacements on Saturday 1 March 

Hurstbridge line (between Heidelberg and Eltham): Passengers returning from the event are advised to board a Heidelberg train at Southern Cross (Platform 9) and change at Heidelberg for a bus. 

Sandringham line, or Malvern, Armadale, Toorak, Hawksburn & South Yarra stations: Passengers are to board either a Clifton Hill / City Circle / Burnley Group train at Southern Cross station (Platforms 9 or 10) and change at Parliament for a bus. 

Frankston, Pakenham & Cranbourne line (From Caulfield and beyond): Passengers returning from the event are advised to board a Burnley Group train at Southern Cross (Platforms 10), then change at Burnley for an express bus to Caulfield.

Sunbury & Craigieburn line from Southern Cross on Saturday 1 March 

Passengers are advised to board at Southern Cross (Platform 14). Services run as normal.
